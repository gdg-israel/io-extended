DevFestFamily is a tech conference for the whole family, whether you are a programmer or not! Our goal is to teach Utahns to better appreciate code. We will have classes and activities for kids, teens, and adults. Even those adults not interested in coding, but want to learn how to protect and guide their family in these highly technical times.

Please bring your family and friends, and let us teach them to code, and get some free lunch we you are at it. Thanks to our generous sponsors, we wil be providing lunch and t-shirts to all who attend. We also will have some amazing door prizes. We are hoping to give away a Raspberry Pi to each family that attends (while supplies last). If you have any questions, please direct them to info@devfestfam.com.

So You Think You Want to Write Code?

Coding can be a lot of fun! In this session we will talk about why coding might be something you will love. There are a lot of cool things you can do with code. We will look at examples and some of them might surprise you. Many resources are available to help you get a jumpstart on learning and most of them are free! We will look at some and provide a good list to take with you. When we are done you should know if you would like to try writing code and how to get started. Did I mention FUN?!

John Woodruff
Provo Web Academy
Provo Web Academy is a 9-week web development training course. Their mission is to allow the learning of web development to be as easily accessible and affordable as possible.
Learning Web Development

Web Development is a skill that is growing increasingly important in today's world. With all aspects of our lives moving more towards the web, it is vital to have a good web development foundation. As the instructor at Provo Web Academy (http://provowebacademy.com) I teach people frequently the basics of web development, giving them a strong foundation in that aspect of technology. Looking to build an online presence for your business or yourself? I'll be teaching you why Web Development skills are very important and helping you to understand that those skills are easily obtainable.

Scratch Programming for Beginners

Scratch is a programming language that was designed to be used by people young and old who have never programmed before. Developed by MIT, it is an easy way to learn the concepts of programming while using drag and drop to create your own interactive stories, games and animations. Scratch also makes it very easy for you to share your creations with others online. Want to make an online and interactive birthday card for your Grandmother? Do you have an idea for a game that you would like to build and share with others? This sessions will give you an overview of what Scratch is, and some ideas for the kind of things that you can do with it.

Learning Programming with Kahn Academy for Kids

Khan Academy has recently added a computer science section that makes learning to write code very interactive. It includes a very innovative way of letting you see code as it is written, and modify it at any time so that you know exactly what everything does. It is free and runs in a browser, so as long as you have a computer with a network connection you will be able to write code and immediately see what it does in the browser. This session will give a quick overview of what Khan Academy has to offer students learning how to program, and some ideas of what it would be like to use this resource to learn how to write your own code.

Arduino Autonomous Vehicle

This session shows how to take an off-the-shelf, inexpensive RC car and combine it with an arduino, sensors and servos to create an autonomous vehicle, all for about $100. The session covers how to hook up the electronics and how to create a simple program for the arduino.

Robotics with FIRST Lego League

Have you ever wanted to build and program your own robot? What if you could enter your robot in a competition that had the feeling of a sports game? FIRST is an acronym, with means "For Inspiration and Recognition of Science and Technology." This organization has a Lego League, where teams of students (up to 10 per team) build and program robots using the Lego Mindstorms kits to complete different challenges in a competition. This session will introduce you to what FIRST Lego League is, and will let you see first-hand what a FLL competition is like. It will also include ideas of how you can join a FLL team, or start one of your own. Come see out what all of the excitement is about!

For True Beginners

In this session we will provide an overview of development and programming for the person who is interested, but knows nothing. We'll describe different programming languages, various development purposes (e.g. mobile devices, websites, personal computers, etc.), and we'll share resources and approaches for getting started in each of these. We'll also cover some suggestions and principles for learning on your own, since most beginners will want to continue learning on their own after leaving this conference.

How to "Hack" the Web

Google Blockly: Visually Programming

Based on the research at MIT (including Scratch), Google Blockly is a set of javascript libraries that makes it possible to write code in Python, or any other language through a visual interface that involves dragging and dropping blocks on the screen. Come try this method of programming and learn about programming concepts without ever having to write a block of code.

Why Attend?

DevFestFamily is not a typical tech conference, as it's focus is on the entire family. With so many jobs being replaced by software, the importance of teaching Utah's children the skills of coding has never been higher. DevFestFamily aims to bring code to those who have never coded, and help train those who are already familiar with coding principles. With this we hope to prepare Utahn's to cope with a job market that is dramatically shifting towards development jobs.
